# CHCC Activity Report — Community in Action
## Pre-Construction Phase: Proving the Community Exists

## Strategic Purpose

*Modeled after the Keshavam Center (Washington D.C.), CHCC's fundraising strategy begins with a simple, powerful truth: the community already exists. Before a single brick is laid, we will demonstrate that CHCC is not a vision for the future — it is a living reality happening today, in Charlotte, in parks and living rooms and rented halls across the city.*

## The Four Pillars — Current Activity Highlights

---

### Pillar 1: PRAY & WELLNESS

**Weekly Community Yoga in Charlotte Parks**
- *Location:* Freedom Park, Reedy Creek Nature Preserve, Romare Bearden Park (rotating monthly)
- *Schedule:* Every Saturday, 8:00–9:00 AM
- *Format:* Open-air, donation-based Hatha Yoga led by certified instructors
- *Audience:* All are welcome — 40–80 participants per session
- *Impact:* 52 sessions per year; 2,500+ individual attendance instances annually
- *Quote:* "I found my people at a park yoga class. I didn't even know CHCC existed — now I volunteer every week." — Priya M., Charlotte

**Online Meditation & Pranayama Sessions**
- *Platform:* Zoom (link shared via CHCC newsletter and WhatsApp community)
- *Schedule:* Tuesdays and Thursdays, 7:00–7:30 AM
- *Format:* Guided Nadi Shodhana, Yoga Nidra, and short contemplative meditation
- *Reach:* 150+ registered participants; average 45 attendees per live session; recordings watched by 200+ monthly
- *Community Highlight:* A Charlotte-area physician leads monthly "Ayurveda Q&A" sessions following meditation — drawing 100+ attendees.

**Vedic Chanting Circle**
- *Schedule:* Every Sunday, 7:00–8:00 AM (in-person + live stream)
- *Venue:* Rotating between member homes and community rooms
- *Participants:* 20–35 per session; 200+ unique participants in past year
- *Texts:* Vishnu Sahasranama, Sundara Kanda, Lalita Sahasranama, Hanuman Chalisa

---

### Pillar 2: LEARN

**Monthly Vedanta & Philosophy Study Groups**
- *Target Audience:* Young professionals (25–45), second-generation Indian-Americans
- *Schedule:* First Sunday of every month, 6:30–8:30 PM
- *Format:* Chapter-by-chapter study of Bhagavad Gita + discussion. Guest speakers quarterly.
- *Attendance:* 25–50 per session
- *Testimonial:* "Growing up here, I felt disconnected from my culture. These study groups gave me a framework for my identity — not religion, but philosophy I can actually live." — Arjun K., Software Engineer

**Pathashala Weekend Classes (Pop-Up Format)**
- *Current Status:* Hindi and Sanskrit classes running in rented community room at a local library.
- *Enrollment:* 35 children; 2 volunteer teachers
- *Parent Demand:* Waitlist of 40+ families pending permanent facility
- *Milestone:* First student recited full Hanuman Chalisa at community event — standing ovation from 200+ attendees.

**STEM + Dharma Workshops (Quarterly)**
- Robotics and coding workshops for ages 8–14, blending technology with values of ahimsa and sustainability.
- Partnerships with Charlotte-area Indian-American engineers who volunteer as instructors.
- 4 workshops held to date; 120 total participants.

**Heritage Arts Pop-Up Classes**
- Bharatanatyam fundamentals for beginners (6-week sessions, twice per year): 25 students enrolled
- Tabla introduction workshops: 3 workshops; 45 participants
- Rangoli art sessions at festivals: 200+ participants

### Pillar 3: GATHER

**Monthly "Meet & Greet" Networking Potlucks**
- *Concept:* Community members bring a dish; CHCC provides the space, music, and programming.
- *Schedule:* Third Saturday of every month
- *Venue:* Rented community halls in South Charlotte, Ballantyne, and Huntersville (rotating to reach all parts of the metro)
- *Average Attendance:* 80–120 per event
- *Feature:* Each potluck highlights a different regional Indian cuisine — Gujarat night, Tamil Nadu night, Maharashtra night — celebrating India's diversity.
- *Testimonial:* "We moved to Charlotte 8 months ago and knew nobody. Our first CHCC potluck was the night Charlotte became home." — Sunita & Rajesh P., Huntersville

**Game Nights — Indian Board Games Revival**
- *Games Featured:* Carrom, Chess (Chaturanga origins acknowledged), Parcheesi (Pachisi origins), Mah Jongg (cross-cultural exchange), Pallanguzhi
- *Schedule:* Second Friday of every month, 7–10 PM
- *Attendance:* 40–60 per event; growing 15% month-over-month
- *Special Feature:* "Grandparent-Grandchild" game night quarterly — explicitly pairing elders with youth.

- Informal 90-minute conversations on topics relevant to the Indian-American community: parenting bicultural children, mental health stigma, career transitions, aging parents.
- Average 30–50 attendees; low-key, living-room format.
- Created 3 ongoing support groups that now meet independently.

### Pillar 4: SERVE (SEWA)

**Adopted Street — Monthly Charlotte Cleanup**
- *Partnership:* Keep Charlotte Beautiful (official "Adopt-a-Street" designation)
- *Location:* CHCC-adopted stretch of Albemarle Road, East Charlotte
- *Schedule:* Second Saturday of every month, 8:00–10:00 AM
- *Volunteers Per Event:* 15–35
- *Total Hours Logged:* 400+ hours of community service
- *Community Impact:* Adopted street is now a visible symbol of the Indian-American community's civic pride.

**Sewa Diwali Food Drive**
- *National Alignment:* Part of the national Hindu Sewa Diwali initiative, which collected 2.5 million pounds of food across 200+ cities in 2023.
- *CHCC's 2024 Result:* 3,200 lbs of non-perishable food collected; distributed to Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina.
- *Volunteers:* 45 volunteers across collection, sorting, and distribution
- *2025 Goal:* 8,000 lbs

**Back-to-School Supply Drive**
- *Partners:* Title I schools in Mecklenburg County (CMS partnership)
- *2024 Result:* 250 complete backpacks with school supplies delivered to 3 partner schools
- *Volunteer Hours:* 120 hours of shopping, packing, and delivery

**Disaster Relief Network**
- Mobilized 50 volunteers within 48 hours during Hurricane Helene relief efforts (October 2024)
- Raised $18,500 in community donations for flood-affected Western North Carolina communities
- Coordinated supply runs to Asheville and surrounding areas

**Senior Wellness Outreach**
- Weekly phone check-ins to 25 isolated Hindu senior community members
- Monthly in-home visits by trained volunteers
- Holiday meal delivery: Diwali prasad delivered to 40 homebound seniors in 2024
