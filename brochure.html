Why CHCC · Why Now

Charlotte's Indian-American
community is thriving —
and underserved.

Over 80,000 Indian-Americans call Greater Charlotte home. Engineers, physicians, educators, entrepreneurs, and their families have built careers and raised children here. Yet this vibrant community gathers in rented church halls, hotel ballrooms, and private backyards — spaces that cannot preserve culture, educate children, or forge the intergenerational bonds that keep Dharmic values alive.

The Charlotte Hindu Community Center changes that. It is not just a building — it is an investment in identity, belonging, and the next generation.

  • Cultural Loss: Second-generation children growing up without language, heritage arts, or philosophical grounding.
  • Wellness Gap: Authentic Ayurvedic, Kalaripayattu, and traditional yoga offerings are nonexistent in the Charlotte market.
  • Senior Isolation: First-generation elders lack dedicated spaces for social connection, language-comfortable support, and cultural continuity.
  • Civic Voice: The Indian-American community lacks a unified anchor institution for civic leadership and community philanthropy.

✦   Proven Models We Are Built Upon

Keshavam (Washington D.C.) — Community first, building second. Their grassroots model raised $3M before breaking ground.  ·  S3 Gaushala (Memphis) — America's premier urban desi cow sanctuary and a model for CHCC's Gaushala.  ·  Jewish Community Centers — The gold standard for multi-generational, multi-faith civic institutions.
