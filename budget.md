## Track C: Community Loan Program
### For Visionary Donors — $50,000 | $100,000 | $150,000

*The Community Loan Program gives major donors maximum financial flexibility. Each of the three loan structures can be taken over a 3-year, 5-year, or 7-year term, giving donors 9 distinct combinations to match their personal financial situation.*

**Core Principle:** Large-scale giving should work for both the donor and the community. These structures allow CHCC to access major capital while protecting the donor's financial interests.

**All loans are activated within 30 days of CHCC's permanent facility being officially identified. All structures governed by a signed pledge agreement.**

---

### Loan Option 1: Nishkama (निष्काम) — Interest-Free Community Loan
*"My capital builds the community. Return it when you're ready."*

**Structure:**
- Donor lends CHCC the pledged amount at 0% interest.
- Full principal returned at end of chosen term (3, 5, or 7 years).
- Repayment schedule: quarterly installments OR single balloon payment — donor's choice.
- Donor may elect to forgive part or all of the balance at any time (converting it to a tax-deductible donation).

**Financial Outcomes:**

| Amount | 3-Year Term | 5-Year Term | 7-Year Term |
|--------|------------|------------|------------|
| $50,000 | Repay $50,000 | Repay $50,000 | Repay $50,000 |
| $100,000 | Repay $100,000 | Repay $100,000 | Repay $100,000 |
| $150,000 | Repay $150,000 | Repay $150,000 | Repay $150,000 |

**Effective Gift (Opportunity Cost at 5%):**
- $50K for 3 years → ~$7,500 effective donation value
- $100K for 5 years → ~$25,000 effective donation value
- $150K for 7 years → ~$52,500 effective donation value

**Tax Treatment:** The loan itself is not tax-deductible. Any forgiven amount is a charitable deduction in the year of forgiveness.

**Ideal For:** Business owners with accessible capital; donors approaching retirement who want capital preserved; community members who believe in the mission but need capital optionality.

---

### Loan Option 2: Moksha Daan (मोक्ष दान) — Graduated Forgiveness Loan
*"I want to donate — but spread the tax benefit intelligently over time."*

**Structure:**
- Donor lends CHCC the pledged amount at 0% interest.
- Each year, an equal portion of the principal is formally "forgiven" — legally converted from a loan to a tax-deductible charitable contribution.
- By term end, 100% of the principal has been donated. CHCC repays $0.
- This is a structured multi-year donation — the loan mechanism allows the donor to spread charitable deductions across multiple tax years.

**Financial Outcomes (100% of principal becomes a tax-deductible donation):**

| Amount | 3-Year Term | 5-Year Term | 7-Year Term |
|--------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Annual deduction — $50,000** | $16,667/yr | $10,000/yr | $7,143/yr |
| **Annual deduction — $100,000** | $33,333/yr | $20,000/yr | $14,286/yr |
| **Annual deduction — $150,000** | $50,000/yr | $30,000/yr | $21,429/yr |
| **CHCC repays at end** | $0 | $0 | $0 |

**Why this is powerful:** IRS rules limit cash donation deductions to 60% of Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) in a single year. A physician donating $100,000 with AGI of $400,000 can deduct the full $100K in one year — but may prefer to spread it across 5 years ($20K/year) for cleaner tax planning. The Moksha Daan loan makes this elegant and automatic.

**Tax Treatment:** Each year's forgiven tranche is a recognized charitable contribution, generating a tax deduction in that fiscal year. Consult your CPA.

**Ideal For:** High-income professionals (physicians, attorneys, tech executives, entrepreneurs) with variable income; donors who want 100% to become a donation but prefer spreading deductions across multiple tax years.

---

### Loan Option 3: Vriddhi (वृद्धि) — Deferred-Interest Ethical Investment
*"I want a fair return — and the knowledge that my capital built something eternal."*

**Structure:**
- Donor lends CHCC the pledged amount at 4% simple interest per annum.
- No payments during the loan term — fully deferred.
- CHCC repays principal + accumulated interest as a single balloon payment at end of term, funded from earned revenue once the facility is operational.
- Donor may elect at any time to convert their loan (principal + accrued interest) into a tax-deductible charitable contribution.

**Financial Outcomes (4% simple interest, deferred):**

| Amount | 3-Year Repayment | 5-Year Repayment | 7-Year Repayment |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| $50,000 | $56,000 (+$6,000) | $60,000 (+$10,000) | $64,000 (+$14,000) |
| $100,000 | $112,000 (+$12,000) | $120,000 (+$20,000) | $128,000 (+$28,000) |
| $150,000 | $168,000 (+$18,000) | $180,000 (+$30,000) | $192,000 (+$42,000) |

**CHCC's Commitment:** The Vriddhi loan is backed by CHCC's projected earned revenue from Aum School tuition ($1.6M/yr by Year 3), Gaushala programs, and wellness memberships. Vriddhi lenders of $100K+ receive a seat on CHCC's Advisory Council and quarterly financial reports.

**Tax Treatment:** Interest earned is taxable income. However, the donor may convert any or all of the loan to a charitable donation at any time — at which point the converted amount becomes a tax deduction.

**Ideal For:** Community members whose idle capital earns below 4%; those who want to "invest" in CHCC as they would invest in a trusted friend's business; candidates for the Advisory Council.

## Donation Mechanics & Financial Controls

**Board Oversight:**
- Finance Committee reviews all expenditures over $5,000
- Independent CPA audits conducted annually
- All audited financial statements published on the CHCC website within 90 days of fiscal year end

**Fund Segregation:**
- Capital Campaign funds held in a separate restricted account
- Earned revenue held in operations account
- Endowment funds professionally managed in a separate investment account (corpus never touched; only earnings used)

**Payment Options (Website Integration):**
- Credit/Debit card (Stripe or Braintree integration; 2.9% + $0.30 processing fee absorbed by CHCC)
- ACH/Bank transfer (no processing fee)
- Check by mail (instructions on donate page)
- Stock/Securities transfer (contact CHCC CFO directly)
- DAF (Donor-Advised Fund) grants accepted
- Cryptocurrency (contact for wallet address)

**Tax Receipts:**
- Automatic email receipt generated immediately for online donations
- Year-end summary statement sent by January 31 for all annual donors
- Special acknowledgment letters (required by IRS for gifts of $250+) sent within 30 days

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is CHCC a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit?**
Yes. CHCC is a federally recognized 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization. Our EIN is provided on all donation receipts.

**Is my donation tax-deductible?**
Yes, to the fullest extent permitted by law. You will receive an acknowledgment letter within 30 days of your donation.

**Can I restrict my donation to a specific program?**
Yes. Donors may designate gifts to specific programs (Aum School, Gaushala, Scholarship Fund, etc.). Designations are honored and reported in annual financial statements.

**What happens if CHCC doesn't meet its fundraising goal?**
If Phase 1 goals are not met within 36 months, restricted donations will be returned to donors upon request, or redirected to an equivalent Dharmic nonprofit with donor consent.

**Can my employer match my donation?**
Many employers match charitable donations. Visit your HR portal or Double the Donation database to check eligibility. CHCC can provide any documentation your employer requires.
