The South Charlotte Market:
A $40M+ Opportunity Untouched

Page 2 of 7
The Community

The Ballantyne–Matthews–Waxhaw corridor is the fastest-growing Indian-American settlement zone in the Southeast. Over 40,000 Indian-American residents live within a 15-minute drive of the proposed CHCC site — a density comparable to the suburbs of Northern Virginia and the Greater Houston corridor, both of which have supported multiple thriving Hindu community centers for decades.

Despite this density, Charlotte has zero dedicated, multi-program Hindu community centers. Events happen in rented church halls, hotel ballrooms, and private driveways. Every cultural program, every yoga class, every Diwali celebration competes for time on a calendar that belongs to someone else.

The Wallet Share Insight: South Charlotte Indian-American households spend an estimated $3,000–$12,000 annually on cultural programming, tutoring, yoga classes, and community events — almost entirely at facilities with no connection to their values. CHCC captures this spending and redirects it into a community-owned institution.
