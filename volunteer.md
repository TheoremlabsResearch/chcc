# Volunteer & Sewa (Selfless Service) Opportunities

---

## Why Sewa?

In the Hindu tradition, *Sewa* — selfless service performed without expectation of reward — is considered one of the highest spiritual practices. Swami Vivekananda said: *"Serve man as God."* At CHCC, volunteering is not just a logistical need — it is the spiritual heartbeat of our institution.

**Every hour you give builds the community you want to live in.**

We welcome volunteers of all faiths, backgrounds, and skill levels. If you have a skill we haven't listed, we want to hear from you.

---

## Volunteer Philosophy

CHCC volunteers are not helpers — they are **co-creators**. We do not place volunteers in boxes. We onboard you, understand your strengths, and match you with roles that are meaningful for *you* and valuable for the community.

All volunteers receive:
- CHCC Volunteer ID card
- Access to free yoga and meditation sessions
- Invitation to volunteer-only quarterly appreciation dinners
- Annual Seva Certificate recognized by CHCC board
- Reference letters for students and early-career professionals

## Volunteer Opportunities by Area

---

### 1. Aum School Support

**Curriculum Development Team**
- Help create culturally rich, STEM-aligned lesson plans that incorporate Vedic Math, Sanskrit phonetics, and Ayurvedic science.
- Roles: Curriculum writers, illustrators, graphic designers, children's book authors
- Commitment: 4–6 hours/month; project-based
- Skills needed: Education background, graphic design, illustration, or passion for learning

**Teaching Assistants (Hindi & Sanskrit)**
- Support lead teachers during weekend Pathashala classes.
- Help students with pronunciation, writing scripts, and comprehension.
- Commitment: 3–4 hours every Saturday; consistent attendance required
- Skills needed: Conversational Hindi or Sanskrit proficiency

**Classroom Volunteers**
- General classroom support: art projects, library reading circles, STEM experiment assistants.
- Commitment: Minimum 2 Saturdays per month
- Background check required for all volunteers working with children.

**Aum School Ambassadors**
- Represent the school at community events, schools fairs, and networking events.
- Distribute enrollment materials and answer parent questions.
- Commitment: Flexible; event-based (5–10 events per year)

---

### 2. Gaushala (Cow Sanctuary) Care

**Weekend Meet & Feed Guides**
- Lead visitor groups through the Gaushala experience: introduce the cows, explain desi cattle breeds, teach feeding techniques, share Panchagavya health information.
- This is CHCC's most popular and emotionally rewarding volunteer role.
- Commitment: Saturdays or Sundays, 8:30–11:30 AM (minimum 2x/month)
- Training provided: 4-hour orientation + shadow shift

**Daily Cow Care Assistants**
- Feeding, grooming, pasture rotation, and basic health monitoring.
- Work alongside the Gaushala manager.
- Commitment: Minimum 3 mornings per week, 7:00–9:00 AM
- Ideal for: Retirees, those working from home, or anyone seeking a meditative, grounding daily practice

**Community Garden & Herb Sanctuary Team**
- Seeding, weeding, watering, composting, and harvesting.
- Monthly "Seva in the Soil" volunteer mornings (open to all Charlotte residents).
- Commitment: 1 scheduled volunteer morning/month + optional additional hours
- No experience needed — training provided.

**Agricultural Education Coordinator**
- Develop and lead field trip curriculum for visiting school groups.
- Partner with Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools to schedule Gaushala field trips.
- Skills needed: Education or public speaking background; knowledge of sustainable agriculture a plus.

---

### 3. Civic Engagement (Sewa Initiatives)

**Street Cleanup Coordinators**
- Organize and lead the monthly CHCC-adopted street cleanup on Albemarle Road.
- Coordinate supplies, safety briefings, volunteer assignments, and photography.
- Commitment: Second Saturday of every month, 7:45–10:30 AM (2.75 hours)
- Skills needed: Basic leadership and coordination; enthusiasm for civic pride

**Sewa Diwali Food Drive Team**
- Coordinate collection points across Charlotte's Indian community (temples, restaurants, businesses, neighborhoods).
- Manage sorting, counting, and delivery to Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina.
- Commitment: Heavy lift in October; planning from August; 20–30 volunteer hours over 3 months.

**Disaster Relief Network Volunteers**
- Be part of CHCC's rapid-response team for regional disasters and humanitarian crises.
- Training: Basic disaster preparedness orientation (provided by CHCC in partnership with American Red Cross).
- Commitment: On-call; activated as needed (1–3 times/year typically)

**School Supply Drive Team**
- Collect, sort, and deliver school supplies to Title I schools in Mecklenburg County.
- Coordinate with CMS school counselors on priority item lists.
- Commitment: August intensive (2–3 weekends); light coordination year-round.

**Senior Wellness Outreach Volunteers**
- Weekly 15-minute phone check-in with an assigned isolated Hindu senior.
- Monthly in-home visit (paired with a partner volunteer for safety).
- Commitment: 2–3 hours/month; consistent, ongoing relationship
- Skills needed: Patience, compassion, and ability to converse in Hindi, Gujarati, Telugu, or Tamil a strong plus.

### 4. Professional Skills Volunteers

**Digital Marketing & Social Media**
- Manage CHCC's Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and LinkedIn channels.
- Create content: reels, stories, event graphics, testimonial videos.
- Write and schedule the weekly CHCC newsletter.
- Commitment: 5–8 hours/week; remote-friendly
- Skills needed: Social media strategy, Canva or Adobe Creative Suite, copywriting

**Event Photography & Videography**
- Document CHCC events, programs, and community gatherings.
- Build the photo/video library for the website's "Our Impact" section and fundraising materials.
- Commitment: Event-based (4–8 events/month as available)
- Skills needed: DSLR or mirrorless camera proficiency; basic video editing

**Website & Technology Volunteers**
- Support the CHCC website team: content updates, bug testing, accessibility audits.
- Developers especially welcome: React, Next.js, Tailwind CSS, CMS experience.
- Commitment: Flexible; project-based or ongoing 2–4 hours/week

**Grant Writers**
- Research and write grant applications to foundations, government agencies, and corporate giving programs.
- Skills needed: Proven grant writing experience; nonprofit finance understanding helpful
- Commitment: Project-based; 20–40 hours per grant application

**Legal Advisors**
- Corporate governance, real estate, employment law, and nonprofit compliance counsel.
- Tax advisors for 501(c)(3) compliance, donor acknowledgment letters, and financial controls.
- Commitment: As-needed; board meetings quarterly; specific projects arise periodically

**Financial Advisors & Accountants**
- Support the CHCC Finance Committee with budgeting, financial projections, and audit preparation.
- CPAs welcome for annual audit support.
- Commitment: Quarterly Finance Committee meetings + audit season (January–March)

**Healthcare Professionals**
- Physicians, nutritionists, and mental health practitioners who can lead monthly health talks.
- Ayurvedic practitioners for free community consultation days.
- Commitment: 1 event per quarter minimum

**Architects & Interior Designers**
- Volunteers with commercial/civic space experience to assist with facility planning.
- Vastu Shastra knowledge a unique and valued asset.
- Commitment: Project-based during Phase 2 facility planning

### 5. Event & Operations Support

**Festival Production Team**
- Stage management, logistics, vendor coordination, volunteer management for CHCC's 12+ annual festivals.
- Commitment: 2–3 weekends per event (planning + execution)

**Kitchen & Catering Volunteers**
- Cook and serve at community potlucks, festivals, and Sewa meals.
- Sattvic kitchen philosophy (no onion, no garlic) — training provided.
- Commitment: Event-based; 4–6 hours per event

**Transportation Network**
- Drive community members (especially seniors) to CHCC events and programs.
- Background check and valid NC license required.
- Commitment: Flexible; coordinate via CHCC volunteer app

## The Ambassador Program
### *"Become a CHCC Ambassador"*

CHCC Ambassadors are our most committed community advocates — the people who carry the CHCC mission into their neighborhoods, workplaces, and social circles.

**Ambassador Responsibilities:**
- Represent CHCC at community fairs, corporate events, and networking gatherings.
- Host "Chai & CHCC" information sessions in their home or workplace (CHCC provides materials).
- Recruit at least 5 new members or donors annually.
- Provide feedback from their community to CHCC leadership.

**Ambassador Benefits:**
- Official CHCC Ambassador designation (badge, digital certificate, LinkedIn badge)
- First access to CHCC events, workshops, and programs
- Annual Ambassador Appreciation Dinner with CHCC board
- Personal impact report: see exactly what your community-building achieved
